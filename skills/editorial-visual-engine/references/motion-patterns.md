# Google Flow Motion Patterns

## Shared constraints

- Silent video, no audio track
- Preserve every word, number, logo, icon, and layout position
- Do not regenerate or rewrite text
- Do not introduce new labels, objects, UI, or decorative marks
- Keep the design readable throughout
- Default duration: 6 seconds
- Use restrained movement suitable for a professional LinkedIn or X post

## Mode 1. Seamless loop

Use for:

- Minimal posters
- Single product or logo pairings
- Small data boards
- Static announcement graphics

Motion grammar:

- Soft highlight pulse
- Gentle line draw
- Small icon float of 2 to 6 pixels
- Cursor blink or terminal glow when relevant
- End on the same visual state as the opening frame

Avoid camera zooms and text entrances.

## Mode 2. Motion poster

Use for:

- Workflows
- Timelines
- Step sequences
- Feature pipelines
- Announcement graphics

Motion grammar:

1. Headline settles with minimal opacity change
2. Connectors draw in the intended reading order
3. Cards receive a short highlight or border pulse
4. Output examples appear through a light mask reveal
5. Final frame holds for at least 0.8 seconds

Text may appear through masks, but letters must not morph, scramble, or change.

## Mode 3. Parallax editorial motion

Use for:

- Multi-layer maps
- Dense org charts
- Central-product diagrams
- Editorial layouts with foreground and background planes

Motion grammar:

- Background grid moves at 5 to 10 percent of foreground speed
- Main cards remain nearly fixed
- Connectors and small illustrations move subtly
- No perspective distortion of text-bearing panels
- Final frame returns close enough to the first frame for a clean loop

## Mode selection

- Choose seamless loop when the static composition is already strong.
- Choose motion poster when order or process is the story.
- Choose parallax editorial motion when depth helps separate layers.

## GIF conversion note

The generated video should have:

- Stable first and last frames
- No audio
- No fast gradients or particle noise
- Limited full-frame movement
- A held final frame

These choices reduce GIF file size and visible looping jumps.
